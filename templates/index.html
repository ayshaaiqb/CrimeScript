Welcome to Crime Scripting







What is Crime Scripting?

In 1994, Derek Cornish introduced the idea of crime scripting as a method to deconstruct complex crimes into smaller and understandable actions that could later be prevented.
This method aims to improve understanding of security analysts about security and crime incidents.

What is the Methodology?

There are 3 types of crime scripting:
- Narrative
- Tables
- Flowcharts



All types of scripts will begin with defining the problem and setting the scene.
This is defined through key terms: Protoscript, Script and Track

> Protoscript is describing a subgroup of the crime that shares similar characteristics (e.g. through robbery)

> Script is further divided into subgroups that possess a clear attribute that have implications for prevention (e.g. robbery from the person)

> Track which is details about the offence that has occurred according to the individual (e.g. subway robbery)

The script then describes the sequence of basic actions, locations and roles that comprise of a crime. This can be broken down into the following:

> Prepartion
> Pre-conditions
> During conditions
> Post-conditions

At each stage of the criminals actions, intervention steps and failure requirements can be the planned in order to mitigate the requirements the criminal needs to fulfil.
